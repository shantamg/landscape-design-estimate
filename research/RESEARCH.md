### Irrigation Sub-Categories

- **Drip irrigation** - Emitter-based systems for plant beds, containers, slopes
- **Spray heads** - Pop-up spray for small turf areas and beds (full/half/quarter circle)
- **Rotor systems** - Pop-up gear-driven for large turf areas
- **Smart controllers** - WiFi-enabled, weather-based controllers
- **Backflow prevention** - Required by code
- **Mainline and lateral piping** - PVC pipe and poly tubing
- **Valves and valve boxes** - Control zones
- **Rain sensors / soil moisture sensors**

### Landscape Lighting Sub-Categories

## 4. Professional Estimate & Contract Best Practices

> **SCOPE CHANGE (Feb 25, 2026):** We are building TWO separate documents:
> - **Document 1: Estimate** -- Clean pricing document only (no terms, no signatures)
> - **Document 2: Contract** -- Separate template referencing the estimate (terms, warranty, signatures)

### Document 1: Estimate (Clean Pricing Only)

The estimate is a pure pricing document. It should be visually clean and professional.

**Sections:**

1. **Company Header**
   - Company name, logo
   - Contact information (phone, email, address, website)

2. **Client & Project Info**
   - Client name and address
   - Project/site address (if different)
   - Estimate number, date prepared

3. **Project Description**
   - Brief narrative description of the project
   - Reference to design plans (if applicable)

4. **Plant Material** (listed FIRST)
   - Line items: botanical name, common name, container size, quantity, unit price, line total
   - Subtotal for plant materials

5. **Labor**
   - Line items: service description, quantity (hours or units), rate, line total
   - Subtotal for labor

6. **Other Materials**
   - Hardscape, soil amendments, mulch, irrigation supplies, etc.
   - Line items: description, quantity, unit, unit price, line total
   - Subtotal for other materials

7. **Design Fee**
   - Rate: $195/hr
   - Hours, total

8. **Installation Supervision & Meetings**
   - Rate: $195/hr
   - Hours, total

9. **Summary**
   - Subtotals by section
   - Tax on materials ONLY (not on labor, design fees, or supervision)
   - Grand total

**What the Estimate does NOT include:**
- No terms and conditions
- No warranty language
- No exclusions
- No payment schedule
- No signature/acceptance block

### Document 2: Contract (Separate Template)

The contract is a mostly-static template with fill-in fields. It references the Estimate by number and date.

**Sections:**

1. **Reference to Estimate**
   - Estimate number and date
   - Total amount from estimate

2. **Payment Schedule (Nancy's specific terms)**
   - Plant material costs due at time of ordering
   - 50% design deposit
   - Hardscape/irrigation materials due at time of ordering
   - Remainder due upon project completion

3. **Terms & Conditions** (friendly, approachable tone)
   - Scope of work references estimate
   - Change order process and pricing
   - Cancellation policy
   - Liability and insurance
   - Dispute resolution

4. **Warranty**
   - 30-day plant warranty (contingent on functioning irrigation)
   - 1-year hardscape warranty

5. **Exclusions**
   - Utility locates / underground obstacle discovery
   - Permit fees (unless stated in estimate)
   - Hauling of excess soil beyond specified amount
   - Work on adjacent properties
   - Ongoing maintenance (unless contracted)
   - Pest/disease treatment post-installation

6. **Change Order Process**
   - How changes are documented and priced
   - Approval requirements

7. **Client Signature / Acceptance**
   - Client signature line
   - Date of acceptance
   - Authorization to proceed statement

### Payment Schedule Reference (Nancy's Model)

| Payment Trigger | Amount |
|---|---|
| Plant material ordering | Full cost of plant materials |
| Design phase start | 50% of design fee |
| Hardscape/irrigation material ordering | Full cost of those materials |
| Project completion | Remaining balance |

### Standard Warranty Language (Nancy's Terms)

- **Plants**: "All plant material is warranted for 30 days from installation date, provided an approved irrigation system is operational and client follows prescribed watering schedule. Warranty covers replacement of plant material only."
- **Hardscape**: "All hardscape work is warranted for 1 year from completion date against defects in workmanship. Warranty does not cover damage from natural settling, tree roots, acts of nature, or modifications by others."

### California-Specific Requirements (for Contract document)

- Contractor's State License Board (CSLB) license number must be displayed
- C-27 Landscaping Contractor license for projects over $500
- Home Improvement Contract requirements for residential work
- 3-day right to cancel for door-to-door sales
- Must provide notice of mechanics lien rights (for projects >$400)

## 5. Existing Tools & Competitors

### Major Landscape Estimating Software

| Software | Price | Best For | Key Strengths | Key Weaknesses |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **LMN** | $297-$557/mo | Small-mid companies, budget-focused | Budget-based estimating, job costing, QuickBooks integration | Steep learning curve, scheduling weak, high price for USD |
| **Aspire** | Enterprise pricing (custom) | Mid-large companies | Real-time data, customizable templates, production-rate kits | Visually dense, steep learning, expensive, complex |
| **Jobber** | $49-$129/mo | Small growing teams | User-friendly, affordable, professional CRM | Less robust estimating, limited for complex projects |
| **SingleOps** | $220-$385/mo | Tree care, recurring maintenance | End-to-end management, good for recurring jobs | Limited for design-build, basic estimating |
| **DynaScape** | Custom pricing | Design-focused firms | Strong design tools, integrated estimating | Estimating less robust than dedicated platforms |
| **PRO Landscape** | Varies | Design + estimate combo | Design rendering + estimating in one tool | May lack depth in either area |
| **ArcSite** | Varies | Field-based estimating | Mobile-first, drawing-based estimates | Limited plant/material databases |
| **Groundplan** | Varies | Takeoff-focused | Aerial measurement, digital takeoff | Narrower feature set |
| **Bolster** | Varies | Remodeling + landscape | AI-assisted estimates | Not landscape-specific |

### Gaps and Opportunities in the Market

1. **Solo designer / small firm gap**: Most tools are built for maintenance companies or large contractors. Solo landscape designers and small design-build firms are underserved. They often use:
   - Excel/Google Sheets with custom templates
   - Word/Google Docs for proposals
   - QuickBooks for invoicing
   - Separate design software (CAD, SketchUp, Realtime Landscape Architect)

2. **Price barrier**: The cheapest adequate tool (Jobber) starts at $49/mo but lacks robust plant databases and landscape-specific estimating. LMN at $297+/mo is prohibitive for solo designers.

3. **Plant database integration**: No major tool offers a comprehensive, pre-populated California plant database with sizing and pricing.

4. **Design-to-estimate workflow**: Disconnect between design software and estimating tools forces double data entry.

5. **Proposal aesthetics**: Most tools generate functional but visually plain estimates. Landscape designers want beautiful, branded proposals that reflect their design sensibility.

6. **Client experience**: Limited tools for client-facing estimate presentation, approval, and e-signature.

7. **Material markup flexibility**: Rigid markup structures that don't accommodate the varied ways designers mark up plants vs. hardscape vs. labor.

8. **Regional pricing**: No tool adapts automatically to regional material and labor costs.

### Common Frustrations Reported by Users

- Software is too complex for simple residential projects
- Poor customer support, slow bug fixes
- Loss of data and frequent errors
- Limited customization of estimate templates
- Non-intuitive interfaces
- High cost relative to features used
- Manual data entry requirements
- Poor mobile experience
- Limited integration with accounting software
- Inflexible line item and category structures

## Key Findings Summary

### For the Nancy Lyons Garden Design Estimate Tool:

> **Updated for two-document scope (Feb 25, 2026)**

1. **Two separate documents simplifies the estimate tool significantly**: The estimate becomes a clean pricing-only document. All legal/contractual language lives in a separate, mostly-static contract template. This means the estimate builder UI can focus purely on line items and math.

2. **Estimate section order matters**: Plant Material comes first (Nancy's workflow), followed by Labor, Other Materials, Design Fee ($195/hr), and Installation Supervision & Meetings ($195/hr). Tax applies to materials only.

3. **Plant material needs special handling**: Container size (1-gal through 48" box), flats, and bare root each have different pricing structures. Markup is typically 2-3x wholesale.

4. **Service categories should be organized hierarchically**: Top-level categories (Planting, Hardscape, Irrigation, Lighting, Grading/Drainage, Structures, Maintenance) with sub-categories and individual line items.

5. **The contract template is mostly static**: Nancy's payment terms (plant material at ordering, 50% design deposit, hardscape/irrigation at ordering, remainder on completion), 30-day plant warranty with irrigation contingency, 1-year hardscape warranty. Only fill-in fields are estimate reference number/date, client info, and total.

6. **The biggest market gap is for solo/small designers**: Existing tools are either too simple (Jobber), too expensive (LMN, Aspire), or not landscape-specific. A tool purpose-built for a solo designer like Nancy could be dramatically simpler and more effective.

7. **Pre-populated plant data is achievable**: Multiple free APIs and databases exist for plant information. California-specific data is available from CalFlora, Calscape, and water district resources.

8. **Material pricing should be updateable**: Prices change seasonally and by supplier. The tool should allow easy price updates while maintaining historical estimates at original pricing.

9. **Proposal presentation matters**: For a designer, the estimate IS a sales tool. It should be beautiful, branded, and professional -- not a plain spreadsheet.
